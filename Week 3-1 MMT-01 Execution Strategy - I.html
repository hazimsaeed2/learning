Execution Strategy I — Orders & the Order Book

SELF-TEACHING ONE-PAGER
Companion: _study.html (prose) · code/ (executed notebook)
THE PROBLEM
You decided to buy. Now what? Your order must survive brokers, routing and a live order book — and the price you get is not the price you saw.
YOUR GOAL
Get filled inside your deadline at a price near (or better than) VWAP, without moving the market against yourself.
THE EDGE
Saving 1 basis point on $200M/week = $1.04M/yr. Execution is alpha.

★ SECTION 1 · THE CORE EXAMPLE — read the book, then walk it

The order book = resting limit orders, highest bid first lowest ask first. The gap = spread = price of impatience.

🔌 WHY — ecosystem & routing

Trader → Broker → Exchange → Clearing. Strategies emit buy/sell (bid/ask) orders: new / modified / cancelled.

Router picks the venue; market adapter speaks its language. Every hop = latency (µs) = money.

📊 HOW — VWAP, the execution report card

Weight each price by volume. Buy below VWAP = good fill; above = overpaid. Exact Excel example:

STEP TABLE ② — VWAP = Σ(px×vol) ÷ Σvol

PriceVolumepx × vol
14.001,10015,400
14.506008,700
14.201,80025,560
14.354005,740
Σ3,90055,400
55,400 ÷ 3,900 =14.205

MVWAP = rolling VWAP → live scorecard through the day.

⏱ HOW — is there enough time?

Slice big orders over time. TTH = shares ÷ (VolRatio × MinVol). VolRatio = .15/.075/.025 (High/Med/Low).

⚠ STOP-ORDER DANGER: stops cluster → demand liquidity when least available → add momentum that destabilises price.

📈 WHY — what really moves price?

Not raw volume — order-flow imbalance (OFI): net bid-vs-ask queue change. Relation is linear, R²≈65% (Cont–Kukanov–Stoikov).

💡 BLENDED EXECUTION: passive agent pegs a limit at the bid (earn spread); aggressive agent uses a discretion price to cross when spread is narrow → stays on schedule.

FAQ edge cases

Reading-only findings

KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. The quote is a book, not a single price.

2. Every order trades price certainty against fill certainty.

3. Execution alpha compounds by basis points at large notional.

4. Thin depth plus one-sided flow is where impact appears.
