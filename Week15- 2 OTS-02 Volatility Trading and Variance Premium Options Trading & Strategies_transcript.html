1️⃣ CORE EXAMPLE — Variance Premium: Why the Market Always Overpays for Volatility Insurance

THE PHENOMENON

William Sharpe: "For predicting future values, historical data appear to be quite useful with respect to standard deviation and virtually useless for expected returns."

Implied Volatility (IV) > Realized Volatility (RV)

→ often enough to study, but the gap varies by product and regime

→ Gap = Variance Premium = the opportunity you are trying to capture

VIX LevelVariance PremiumInterpretation
Low VIXHIGH premium (%)Best opportunity! Market overpays most when calm
High VIXLOW or NEGATIVEMarket can't overpay enough — realized catches up

Counter-intuitive: The best vol-selling opportunities are when markets are CALM, not volatile. 2017 (avg VIX = 11.1) was excellent for variance premium trading.

WHY NAIVE SELLING FAILS

Idea: Since IV > RV always, just sell straddles/strangles every day for a decade?

StrategyBacktestReality

⚠️ THREE REASONS IT FAILS:

1. Costs not included — 20–30% of profits eaten by bid-ask spread, brokerage, taxes

2. Drawdowns during crises — must maintain margin during 25–50% drawdown

3. No timing logic — selling when IV ≈ RV (no gap) wastes capital

💡 THE RIGHT APPROACH: Don't sell blindly every day. Identify WHEN the gap is extreme (IV ≫ RV), understand WHY premium exists (insurance demand), and THEN deploy your combination. OTS 3–6 builds this skill.

3️⃣ ADVERSE SELECTION & COSTS — Why Your Beautiful Backtest Will Lie to You

ADVERSE SELECTION

Problem: ML algo predicts extreme lows → places limit orders at bottoms → but almost NO sellers exist at extremes (candle wicks = low volume).

Backtest says: "Bought at $205.90" → Profit!

Reality: Nobody sold you at $205.90. Order never filled.

THE TRAP:

Losing trades can fill more easily

Winning trades can be the ones that never fill

Backtest can't distinguish good vs bad fills

If you get a fill TOO quickly → counterparty is EAGER to sell to you → they might know something → BAD trade.

COSTS (The Alpha Killer)

Cost TypeImpact
Bid-ask spreadLargest hidden cost — can't quantify exactly
SlippageWant 100, filled at 102 (market order reality)
Taxes + brokerageFixed per-trade cost
Platform/data feesSubscription costs
Total impact20–30% of backtest profits GONE

Example: Student got Sharp Ratio 1.45 → added transaction costs → CAGR dropped to −2%!

80% of strategies become WORTHLESS after including costs.

PRACTICAL SOLUTIONS

1. Take ALL trades your model gives → at least 60-70% will fill → some alpha survives

2. Discount brokers — lower brokerage, lower platform fees

3. DON'T use leverage to "save capital" — it MULTIPLIES costs

4. Be aggressive on entries (momentum) but conservative on hedging entries → save cost on hedges

5. Factor costs into backtest — if $100 profit, deduct $20-30 for costs

⚠️ SOCIAL MEDIA RETURNS ARE LIES: Screenshots are cherry-picked outliers. They don't show 200× leverage (crypto), or that 3000% gains come with 500% losses. PnL screenshots can even be faked. Only bank on YOUR live trading results.

4️⃣ FOUR STATISTICAL PROPERTIES OF VOLATILITY — The Foundation of Every Vol Strategy

PROPERTY 1: Negative Correlation with Returns

TimeframeS&P 500 vs VIX Correlation
Daily−0.77
Weekly−0.77
Monthly−0.7

Stock UP → volatility DOWN. Stock DOWN → volatility UP. Consistent across timeframes for equity indices.

But NOT universal: Energy commodities = negative. Gold/silver = POSITIVE (safe haven → price up when fear up → vol up too).

PROPERTY 2: Vol of Vol (VVIX) Positively Correlated (+0.8)

High vol → high vol-of-vol: When volatility is extreme, changes in volatility are ALSO extreme. High chance of vol CRASHING.

Low vol → low vol-of-vol: When volatility is calm, it stays calm. Very low chance of sudden vol spike.

TRADING IMPLICATION:

VIX high (e.g., April 2025) → bet on vol CRASH ✅

VIX low (e.g., Jan 2025) → vol stays low ✅

VIX low → bet on vol SPIKE ❌ (very unlikely)

PROPERTY 3: Mean Reversion

Volatility is strongly mean-reverting. Unlike stocks (which can trend), VIX behaves much more like a mean-reverting series.

TimeframeAutocorrelationDirection Split
DailyWeak negative51% positive / 49% negative
WeeklyStronger negativeMore mean-reversion evident
MonthlyStrongest negative60% positive / 40% negative

Key: Mean reversion is WEAK daily (not tradeable) but STRONG weekly/monthly (tradeable). Don't create daily VIX trading strategies.

PROPERTY 4: Different Regimes

Don't define markets as just "bull" or "bear." Define REGIMES based on vol-return correlation:

Regime A: correlation −1.0 to −0.5 (normal equity)

Regime B: correlation −0.5 to 0.0 (transition)

Regime C: correlation 0.0 to +0.5 (abnormal)

Regime D: correlation +0.5 to +1.0 (commodities/crisis)

Test your strategy in EACH regime. If profitable only in Regime A → deploy only when correlation is in that range.

⚠️ VIX HISTORICAL COMPARISON TRAP: Don't compare current VIX to 1999 (avg 24.4). Since VIX futures/options launched, its behavior CHANGED. Use 3–5 year window only. 2017 avg was 11.1 — context matters.

Who definesMathematics (objective)Market participants (subjective)

💡 CRITICAL DISTINCTION: In options context, when anyone says "volatility" they mean IMPLIED volatility. IV is one of the VERY FEW forward-looking metrics in all of finance. It's like a real-time market survey of what participants expect.

How IV is calculated: BSM model normally takes inputs (incl. HV) → outputs premium. But you ALREADY KNOW the premium (market price). So reverse it: input premium → output = IV. The volatility "implied" by market prices.

IV SURFACE: Smile + Term Structure

IV is NOT one number. For S&P 500, there are hundreds of options contracts (different strikes × different expiries). Each has its OWN IV.

PlotX-AxisY-AxisName
2DStrike priceIVVolatility Smile (or Skew/Smirk)
2DDays to expiryIVTerm Structure
3DStrike × ExpiryIVVolatility Surface

REASONS FOR THE SMILE:

1. Price-vol inverse relationship: Lower strike → price dropped → vol increased → higher IV

2. Leverage effect: Price drop changes company fundamentals (P/E, D/E ratios) → more risk

3. OTM demand: Cheap OTM options attract speculators → increased demand → higher premium → higher IV

REASONS FOR TERM STRUCTURE:

Events cluster at certain dates: earnings in 3 weeks → monthly IV > weekly IV. Weather report for agri commodities → seasonal IV changes.

⚠️ COMMON MISTAKE: Going to Google, noting ONE IV number for a stock. That's only the ATM, near-expiry IV. Different strikes and expiries have COMPLETELY different IVs. Always study the full surface.

6️⃣ OPTIONS PAYOFF FUNCTIONS — Call, Put & Straddle Mechanics

CALL OPTION PAYOFF

Payoff = max(S − X, 0)

S = underlying price at expiry

X = strike price

Profit = Payoff − Premium paid

If S > X: You make (S − X) per contract

If S ≤ X: You get ZERO (lose entire premium)

Key difference from stocks: If Apple drops from $100 to $80, your stock is still worth $80. If your call option expires OTM, it's worth $0. Binary outcome.

Example: Strike = $100, premium = $2, expires at $140 → Payoff = $40, Profit = $38

PUT OPTION PAYOFF

Payoff = max(X − S, 0)

Profit when underlying FALLS below strike

If S < X: You make (X − S) per contract

If S ≥ X: You get ZERO (lose entire premium)

PAYOFF ≠ PROFIT: Payoff is theoretical — what the contract pays at expiry. Profit subtracts the premium you paid. If premium = $2, you only profit when payoff > $2.

STRADDLE (Vol Trade Foundation)

Buy ATM Call + Buy ATM Put

Same strike, same expiry

Cost = Call premium + Put premium

If market MOVES a lot (either direction): You profit from whichever leg gains.

If market DOESN'T move: Both expire worthless → lose both premiums.

Risk: NOT directional risk — it's VOLATILITY risk. If vol drops, you lose.

💡 WHEN TO DEPLOY: Not "anytime you don't know direction." Only when you believe vol will SHIFT significantly (e.g., pre-earnings uncertainty, geopolitical event). The cost of both premiums is high — market must move beyond combined premium cost.

7️⃣ WHY OPTIONS EXIST & MARKET MICROSTRUCTURE — Complexity vs Simplicity

WHY TRADE OPTIONS INSTEAD OF STOCKS?

The paradox: Simple assets (stocks, futures) → traders make them COMPLEX (spoofing, stacking, stop hunting). Complex assets (options) → trading them is actually SIMPLE if you have a clear objective.

Market ScenarioStocksOptions
Market goes upBuy → ProfitBuy call → Profit
Market goes downShort → ProfitBuy put → Profit
Market goes CRAZY (volatile)CAN'T DO ANYTHINGStraddle → Profit!
Market stays FLATCAN'T DO ANYTHINGShort-vol structures CAN profit if premium is rich enough

Options give you the ability to profit from ANY view — not just direction, but volatility, time decay, convexity. That's why professionals trade options.

SPOOFING (Market Manipulation Example)

Depth of Market: Bids (buyers) on left, asks (sellers) on right.

500K order placed at $13.15 → everyone sees huge demand

→ Sellers retreat → price rises to $14.00

→ Spoofer REMOVES $13.15 order and SELLS at $14.00

Spoofing, stacking, flipping, stop hunting = all ILLEGAL (exchange fines). Options can have their own microstructure issues too, but the lecture's point was that basic options views are expressed more directly through the contract structure itself.

WHY VARIANCE PREMIUM EXISTS (Insurance)

Options were originally created for hedging, not speculation. People buy options to INSURE their stock positions — just like insurance premiums in real life.

THE INSURANCE ANALOGY:

Long Apple stock ($2M) → buy Apple puts

Why? Black swan events (COVID, terrorism, war)

→ Constant demand for puts = constant premium

→ IV stays above RV = variance premium

Different assets have different premiums:

OilHigher variance premium (more geopolitical risk)
GoldModerate premium
S&P 500 top stocksLower premium (more efficient)

⚠️ COUNTER-INTUITIVE: Stock goes up ≠ call goes up. Options premium is driven by volatility MORE than direction. If vol drops while stock rises, your call can LOSE value. This is why beginners lose money — they trade options like stocks.

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. The variance premium (IV − RV) exists because options serve as insurance — hedgers constantly overpay for protection against black swans. This gap is your edge, but capturing it requires timing (trade when gap is extreme) not blind daily selling.

2. Volatility has four key properties: negatively correlated with equity returns (−0.77), positively correlated with vol-of-vol (+0.8), mean-reverting (stronger monthly than daily), and regime-dependent. High vol → high chance of crash. Low vol → stays low.

3. Implied volatility is NOT a single number — it forms a 3D surface across strikes and expiries. The smile/skew tells you WHICH contracts are expensive. Term structure tells you WHEN the market expects events. Study the FULL surface, not just ATM near-expiry IV.

4. Your backtest lies: adverse selection means the trades that look best on paper may be the ones that never fill, while bad fills can happen more easily. Costs consume 20–30% of profits. 80% of strategies become worthless after costs. Always factor in bid-ask spread, slippage, and adverse fills before going live.

THE MASTER FORMULA: Options Edge = Variance Premium (IV − RV) + Timing (deploy when gap is extreme) + Cost Management (discount broker, aggressive entries, conservative hedges) + Vol Regime Awareness (4 properties).

THE ONE THING TO NEVER FORGET: In options, "volatility" always means IMPLIED volatility — it's forward-looking, defined by market participants, and forms a surface (not a single number). You're not predicting price direction; you're predicting the CHANGE in volatility. That's what separates options traders from stock traders.
