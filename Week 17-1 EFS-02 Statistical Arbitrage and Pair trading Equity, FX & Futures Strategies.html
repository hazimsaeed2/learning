Statistical Arbitrage & Pairs Trading

Buy what is statistically cheap, sell what is statistically dear — then wait for the spread to revert. Stationarity → ADF test → mean-reversion on one asset, then cointegration → hedge ratio → trading a fabricated stationary spread (pairs).

DIRECTIONAL
1 instrument

Long/short a single price. "Where is corn headed?" Needs a stationary series to mean-revert.

PAIRS
2 instruments

Long one, short the other simultaneously (Google vs Meta). Trade the spread, not the level.

TRIPLET / N-LEG
3+ instruments

Add Amazon → a cointegrated basket. Same maths, more legs (e.g. GLD/GDX/USO).

PART 1 — ONE STATIONARY ASSET

① What is stationarity?

A series is stationary if it keeps returning to a fixed mean (the "Sports Illustrated jinx" — great form reverts to average). Trends (Apple ↑, Nike ↓) and the random walk are not stationary.

② ADF test (Augmented Dickey-Fuller)

ΔPt = λ · Pt−1 + εt
λ = 0 (null H₀)ΔP = noise → random walk → NOT stationary
λ < 0 (alt H₁)inverse pull to mean → stationary

λ = regression slope of ΔP on Pt−1;   t = λ / SE,   SE = σ/√n.

Rule: t-stat more negative than the critical value ⇒ reject H₀ ⇒ stationary.

PART 2 — BUILD A STATIONARY ASSET (PAIRS)

Few real series stay stationary for long. Trick (Nunzio Tartaglia, Morgan Stanley, mid-1980s; later D.E. Shaw): combine two trending ETFs — EWA (Australia) & EWC (Canada) — into a stationary spread. Both are energy economies, so they move together.

⑥ Hedge ratio — OLS through the origin

90 days? avoid look-ahead bias — h must not peek at the back-test window.
no constant? line through origin: if the miner → 0 the metal → 0; and one fewer parameter = less overfit.

spread = EWC − 1.48·EWA

⑧ Trading the spread — direction & quantity

The spread is not tradable directly — place each leg. Long spread: buy 1·EWC, sell h·EWA. Short: reverse.

Quiz: spread = GLD − 3·GDX + 0.5·USO, signal = SHORT. Flip signs, ×2 to clear fraction:

GLDSHORT2 shares
GDXLONG6 shares
USOSHORT1 share

⑨ Capital-normalised returns

ret = ΔSpread / (1·EWC + h·EWA)

Divide by the cash to buy one leg + margin to short the other — NOT by the spread itself (that over-states it).

spread5 → 3 ⇒ ΔS = −2
capital15 + 10 = 25

Two drifting random walks: corr = +0.95 but spread ADF −2.22 (wanders) ⇒ correlated, NOT cointegrated. Correlation alone loses money; add it only as a filter on top of cointegration.

⑫ Picking a pair (before any ADF test)

Cointegration is fragile — there are millions of possible pairs but few stay cointegrated. Narrow the field with economic logic first, then confirm with ADF:

ETFs / FXexposed to common economic factors (EWA & EWC = both commodity economies)
Stockssame sector, similar market-cap, similar ratios
thenADF cointegration check on the spread

Why it works: a cointegrated pair is mean-reverting; once the spread diverges, the probability of reversal rises — and for same-sector names, extreme divergence is rare.

⑭ Normal arbitrage vs statistical arbitrage (+ FAQ gotchas)

Normal arbStatistical arb
whatsame asset, two venuesdifferent assets, statistical link
exampleTCS ₹X on NSE vs ₹X+1 on BSEEWC vs 1.48·EWA spread
riskriskless lockprobabilistic — can break

Non-stationary asset? trade its returns, or build a spread from 2+ assets, or take a log transform.

FX pairs? account for overnight interest (carry) plus commissions & slippage for a realistic back-test.

Triplets? regress each leg against the target stock to get its own hedge ratio.

Cointegration or correlation? for stat-arb you need the spread to be stationary → cointegration, not correlation.

⑮ Risk management & the LTCM case study

Stat-arb is not pure arbitrage — the spread can diverge (FDA alert on one pharma leg, a blow-out earnings beat…).

  • stop-loss: fixed $ / % / std-multiple / ATR
  • diversify across many pairs & strategy styles
  • keep capital buffers — stay solvent
  • retire a pair once it breaks a band never seen in back-test + paper-trade
