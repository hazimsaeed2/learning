VaR — three ways

The 5th-percentile loss. "8% chance of a 2% decline in a month" is a VaR statement.

Method1-day 95% VaR ($1m book)
Historical (empirical pct)$24,957
Parametric (z·σ, z=−1.645)$25,252
Monte Carlo (200k sims)$25,270
CVaR = average loss inside the worst 5% = $31,612 = 1.27× VaR. Captures fat tails VaR ignores.

Limits — geography & settlement

ABC 50% US revenue, 30% US tariff → −15% total revenue hit. Limit: 30% US / 30% EU / 40% RoW.

Futures-vs-cash trap: option +$5,000 (paper, at expiry) vs futures −$5,000 (cash debited tonight via MtM). Net P&L $0 but a margin call now may force unwinding the winning leg.

Other limits: settlement/pinning at the strike · put-call-parity assignment uncertainty · counterparty by rating · orders/day · mandatory 2-week leave & team rotation · centralized PnL reconciliation.

Porsche / VW squeeze (2008)

Porsche held shares + calls = 74%; Lower Saxony 20% → free float ~6%. Shorts forced to cover sent VW €200 → €1005, briefly the world's most valuable company.

Why exchanges cap derivative OI vs free float and set market-wide position limits.
