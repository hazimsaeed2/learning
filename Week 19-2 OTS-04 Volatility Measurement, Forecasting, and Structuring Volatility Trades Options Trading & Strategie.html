Volatility — Measurement, Forecasting & Structuring Vol Trades

There is no true volatility — only estimators. Measure it, forecast it, contextualise it, then harvest the variance premium (IV > RV) with weekend, earnings and covered-call structures. The whole P&L collapses to one equation: PnL = Vega × (IV − RV).

HISTORICAL · HV
The past, definite, known. Std of past returns. Nobody disputes it.
IMPLIED · IV
The future. Backed out of an option's traded premium via Black-Scholes. What the market expects.
REALIZED · RV
Vol actually realized over a contract's life (a range/period idea — not "present" vol).

1 Measuring volatility — only estimators

Whatever number you get is an estimate scattered around the (unknowable) truth. More data points → less error. There is no best method, only better ones — so pick one and stay with it.

estimatorinputs / daynote
Close-to-closeCstd of log returns. Treats sample as population → flawed.
ParkinsonN, H, Lrange-based, more precise; biased LOW (no overnight).
Garman-KlassO, H, L, C4 points → less error.
Rogers-SatchellO,H,L,C +drifthandles a drift term.
Yang-Zhang+ jumpsincludes overnight jumps.
σ_Park = √( (1 / 4N·ln2) · Σ ln(Hᵢ/Lᵢ)² )
Real AAPL 2023: close-to-close 1.252%/day (19.9%/yr) vs Parkinson 1.122%/day (17.8%/yr) — ratio 0.90, the "biased low" story.

3 Forecasting — rolling → EWMA → GARCH

30-day rolling breaks: one +16% day (Netflix 501→586) makes vol jump & crash:
vol before spike21.4%
vol after spike49.6% ↑
vol +30 days (spike exits)16.1% ↓ crash
EWMA weights recent data → smooth decay (62.7% → 48.8% → 29.0%). λ ∈ [0.90, 0.99].
σ²ₜ = (1−λ)·r²ₜ₋₁ + λ·σ²ₜ₋₁

4 GARCH(1,1) = the 3 vol characteristics

σ²ₜ = γ·V + α·r²ₜ₋₁ + β·σ²ₜ₋₁    (α+β+γ = 1)
termvol characteristic
β·σ²ₜ₋₁clusters — high vol → stays high (VIX 15-20 calm / 20-30 stress)
α·r²ₜ₋₁recent returns — current conditions matter
γ·Vmean-reverts — current value already seen in the past
Trader shortcut: assume α ∈ [0.01, 0.1], β ∈ [0.9, 1], then γ = 1 − α − β (don't run full max-likelihood). Contextualise: rank forecast at 20/40/60/80/100-day percentiles.

3 Forecasting trap: rolling window vs EWMA (real NFLX)

A 30-day rolling window keeps a shock for exactly 30 days, then drops it — spike → plateau → cliff-crash. NFLX earnings jump +17.4% (2016-10-18):

EWMA: σ²ₜ = (1−λ)·r²ₜ₋₁ + λ·σ²ₜ₋₁   (λ 0.90–0.99)
Rolling whipsaws you in/out; EWMA lets the shock decay smoothly — the intuition GARCH formalises.

5 Variance premium & the master equation

IV > RV ~77% of the time — people overpay for insurance. Sell the option rich (IV), it settles on the cheaper RV → capture the gap. P&L = premium(IV) − premium(RV); Taylor-expand →

PnL = Vega × (IV − RV)
Vega (per 1 vol-pt)0.1375
Vega × (IV−RV), IV=30% RV=24%0.825
exact premium(IV) − premium(RV)0.825 ✓

7 Covered call + "Vega wounds, gamma kills"

Covered call = long underlying + sell a call (e.g. buy stock, sell 100-call for $3). Upside capped, keep premium. BXM ETF (S&P, 1993-2022): 7.1% vs index 9.4%, lower vol, better Sharpe/drawdown.

Vega wounds, gamma kills. A wrong directional (gamma) short can run to ∞ (short 241 → 560). A wrong vol (Vega) short only wounds — vol mean-reverts so the move tends to reverse. Still must close by expiry.

9 Earnings — IV run-up, crash & implied jump

IV rises into earnings (split opinion), crashes after. Two trades: (a) buy straddle ~10 days before, close day before (~43% win, wins 1.7× losses); (b) capture the IV jump via front-week vs second-week contracts.
