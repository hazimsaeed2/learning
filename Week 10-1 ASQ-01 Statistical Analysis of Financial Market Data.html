STATISTICAL ANALYSIS OF MARKET DATA — AR · STATIONARITY · ACF/PACF · ARIMA

EPAT Week 10-1 · ASQ-01 (Part 1) · Advanced Statistics for Quant Strategies

THE PROBLEM
Simple moving-average rules look great — until a regime shift (COVID, war, rate hikes) arrives and they collapse.
YOUR GOAL
Build adaptive, statistical models — AR → ARIMA — that re-fit as conditions change instead of using fixed rules.
THE EDGE
Model stationary returns, diagnose with ACF/PACF + ADF, forecast one step ahead on a rolling window.

⭐ CORE EXAMPLE — the thread running through this lecture

Take a liquid equity index (lecture: SPY daily + PayPal weekly; here NIFTY, 961 daily / 203 weekly bars, 2017–2020 incl. the COVID crash). ① A MA(10/30) crossover trails buy-and-hold. ② So we get systematic: an AR(1) on returns (they're stationary; prices aren't). ③ Diagnose lags with ACF/PACF. ④ Run a rolling AR(1) forecast. ⑤ It is right 54% of the time yet still loses money — too simple. ⑥ Generalise to ARIMA(p,d,q).

① Why simple rules fail

MA crossover: long if SMA10 > SMA30 else short; shift signal 1 bar (no look-ahead) × returns.

StrategyTotal return
MA(10/30) crossover+26.0%
Buy & hold+47.3%
💡 The rule is smooth in calm regimes but takes brutal hits when volatility spikes (crashes) — it can't adapt. That pain motivates a statistical model.

② AutoRegressive AR(1)

The hunch "up follows up" made systematic — regress a series on its own past value:

r_t = α + β·r_(t-1) + ε_t
βMeaning
0 < β < 1Trending — positive follows positive
−1 < β < 0Mean-reverting — positive follows negative
β ≈ 0Yesterday tells you nothing

AR(p) uses the last p values. We fit on returns, not prices →

③ Stationarity = stability

A series is stationary if its mean & std stay constant over time. Prices trend → non-stationary; returns wiggle around 0 → stationary. That's why we model returns (or differenced prices).

ADF test (Augmented Dickey-Fuller)

Null = non-stationary. Read the p-value only:

Seriesp-valueVerdict
Weekly prices0.099non-stationary
Weekly returns0.0001STATIONARY

p < 0.05 → stationary  |  p ≥ 0.05 → not

④ ACF & PACF — diagnose the lags

ToolMeasures
ACFcorr(r_t, r_(t−k)) — direct + indirect links
PACFdirect only — strips out intermediate lags
Bars poking outside the blue band (±1.96/√N ≈ ±0.138 here) = real autocorrelation worth modelling. Use ACF→q, PACF→p.

⑤ Rolling AR(1) strategy

for t in range(100, N):           # 100-week window
    fit  = ARIMA(r[t-100:t],
                 order=(1,0,0)).fit()
    pred = fit.forecast(1)[0]      # 1 step ahead
    sig  = +1 if pred > 0 else -1 # long / short

Re-fit every week, forecast one step → a true rolling forecast, the professional pattern.

⚠️ Right 54% of the time, yet still lost money. Asymmetric payoffs (losers bigger than winners). The same model flips between out/under-performing across data pulls — unstable. AR(1) alone = a single lag = too simple for production.

📊 The four diagnostics at a glance

① MA rule trails B&H · ② ACF of weekly returns (mostly inside band) · ③ rolling AR(1) loses to B&H · ④ deep AR(1) drawdown

⑥ ARIMA(p, d, q) = AR + Integrated + MA-of-errors

LetterNameWhat it adds
AR(p)AutoRegressiveuses the last p values of the series
I(d)Integrateddifferences the series d times → makes it stationary
MA(q)Moving-avg of errorsmodels the last q forecast errors (pred − actual) to self-correct
⚠️ This MA has nothing to do with the SMA/LMA price moving-averages from the crossover. Here "moving average" = the moving average of the model's errors.

The exam analogy. If a student systematically under-predicts their score by ~2 marks each week, use that recurring error to fix the next prediction. ARIMA's MA term learns from the pattern in past mistakes. Choose (p,d,q) with ADF→d, PACF→p, ACF→q.

⏱️ 30-second glossary

Regime shift — fundamental change in how the market operates (pandemic, war, rate cycle). Breaks fixed rules.
Stationary — mean & std roughly constant over time. Returns yes, prices no.
ADF test — checks stationarity. p < 0.05 → stationary.
ACF — autocorrelation incl. indirect links. PACF — direct only.
AR(p) — regress on own past values. Rolling forecast — re-fit each step, predict 1 ahead.
ARIMA(p,d,q) — AR + differencing + MA-of-errors. The order-selection craft = Part 2.
