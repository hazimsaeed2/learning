THE EDGE

CORE EXAMPLE — market impact = temporary + permanent

A big buy lifts the price while you trade; when you stop it reverts — but not fully. The leftover is information; the snap-back is the liquidity premium.

The trader's dilemma

Minimise Cost(t) = MI(t) + λ·TR(t)

  • Market impact ↓ with time (leak it out gently)
  • Timing risk ↑ with time (more drift exposure)
  • λ = risk aversion (urgency dial)
λstyleoptimal horizon
0.25risk-neutral3.40 days (slow)
1.0balanced0.85 day
3.0risk-averse0.28 day (fast)
💡 closed form t* = a/(λ·b); risk-averse → trade faster.

Algorithm families

  • Execution-only — trade a PM's pre-decided order (the big one).
  • Quant / profit-seeking — decide and execute (stat-arb, signals).
  • AMM / liquidity (HFT) — earn the spread + rebates; sniff out big orders.
  • DMA — broker connectivity, client's own algos.
By aggressiveness: aggressive (market orders) · working (VWAP/TWAP/POV/IS, balance cost-risk) · passive (limit orders, dark pools).
Usage: VWAP ~30% · POV ~25% · liquidity-seeking ~25% · rest customised.
