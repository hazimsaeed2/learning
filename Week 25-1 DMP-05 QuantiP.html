Betting Against Beta (the anomaly)

CAPM: E[R] = r_f + β(R_m − r_f)
r_f≈0 ⇒ E[R] ≈ β·R_m ⇒ higher β = higher expected return

Everyone chases high beta to juice returns, but is leverage-constrained (retail can't borrow 3×). Demand ⇒ high beta overpriced, low beta underpriced. So go long low-beta / short high-beta.

Two biases that wreck this backtest

Survivorship bias

Using today's index members back-tests only winners (delisted/removed names ignored). Fix: use the index's historical constituents at each point in time (Nifty rebalances half-yearly via NSE press releases).

Look-ahead bias

Computing beta on the full sample uses future data you wouldn't have. Fix: estimate beta on a training window (≤ 2017) only, then trade 2018+. Rebalance (recompute beta) monthly/quarterly via a date-driven loop.

Why low beta wins in crashes (the math): a 10 % fall needs an 11 % gain to recover; a 50 % fall needs 100 %. Low-beta names fall less, so they recover faster — the engine of BAB's out-performance.
