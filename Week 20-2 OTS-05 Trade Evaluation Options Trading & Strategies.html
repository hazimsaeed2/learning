Trade Evaluation, Hedging & a Volatility Case Study

A volatility trade's edge is PnL ≈ Vega × (IV − RV) — no price term. So we delta-hedge the price out, but not continuously (cost!). Then we evaluate the strategy on the shape of its equity curve, and run a full SPY case study end to end.

2 Why not hedge continuously?

Re-hedging every tick = infinite transaction cost + fighting the market. Simulate a delta-hedged short call with no vol edge (IV=RV):

mean PnL≈ −0.07 (cost drag)
trades that LOSE money~54%
Hedging is mandatory for a vol trade — but doing it every day is self-defeating. We must hedge optimally.

3 Whalley–Wilmott delta bands (utility-based hedging)

Don't force delta to 0 — keep it inside a band, hedge only when it leaves. Half-width:

Δband = ( 3·e−r(T−t)·λ·S·Γ² / 2κ )1/3
  • λ proportional cost — Apple \$130/\$1 → 1/130; index \$100/0.05 → 0.0005
  • Γ² gamma squared = the dominant driver (band fattens near the money)
  • κ risk aversion — your personality; calibrate once (lecture ~0.24), reuse everywhere
band stylehedgescosttrader
narrowmanyhighrisk-averse
widefewlowrisk-tolerant
At a 0.6 call delta you keep the book inside e.g. [0.57, 0.69] rather than hedging all the way to 0 — that saves the cost of needless trades.

7 Closing — exercise & the dividend rule

80-strike call on \$100 stock worth 20.10 = \$20 intrinsic + \$0.10 time value. A \$1 dividend drops stock to \$99:

exercise early (19 + \$1 div)20.00
hold19.10
Exercise early when dividend > extrinsic. Also: pinning (settles near ATM strike → pin risk), cash vs physical settlement, European = nullify/expiry only.

8 Evaluating a strategy — beyond cumulative return

A good trade is repeatable & consistent, not one big number — and no single metric suffices. The lecture's two strategies show why:

9 Case study — selling SPY options (3 Jun 2019)

  1. Expiration: 1m/3m/6m all ATM IV ≈ 18.8% → pick 1-month (shorter-dated = higher vol, validate fast).
  2. Structure: short strangle — sell 262 put + 286 call (ATM 275); cheaper edges than a straddle.
  3. Tail hedge: buy 100 of the 232 puts (2-delta) to cap a 30% crash.
  4. Hedging vol: forecast realized — VIX 14.1, GARCH 13.7 → avg 13.9%.
