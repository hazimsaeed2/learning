Trading paradigms = latency sensitivity

Forget the textbook split. Define by how fast your edge decays. There is no progression LFT→MFT→HFT — each is its own business.

ParadigmEdge survivesEdge dies if you add
LFT low~100 ms+ delay(not latency-bound)
MFT mida few msa few 100 ms
HFT higha few µsevery extra µs

A retail broker API round-trips in ~30 ms; an FPGA stack in 0.5 µs → retail is 60,000× slower. Use "HFT" carefully.

Market access — own membership vs broker

Own membership pros: low latency · control · IP protection · no counter-party risk (iRage's own clearer once neared insolvency → they took clearing membership) · capital efficiency. Cons: membership fee + heavy compliance.

4 membership tiers: Prop/Alpha (own book, no clients, lowest compliance) · Trading (clients, clear via a CM) · Clearing (clients + self-clear) · PCM (clears only, no trading). Rule: buy membership only when cost savings are 2-3× the membership + compliance burden.

Bank-guarantee multiplier (worked):

Cost crossover: below ~2,069M orders/yr a broker is cheaper; above it, own membership wins. A mid-tier HFT sends 10-100M orders/day — far past break-even.

Co-location — fair, finite, power-bound

Same network as the matching engine. Same building but different network = only proximity hosting. Fairness = equal cable length to every rack (distance in the hall is irrelevant).

Order-routing budget — messages, not orders

Exchanges throttle messages/s (new + cancel + modify). CME = 1,500 / 3-s window (500/s); NSE = 1,000/s per line.

Exchange revenue = order fees + (colo or market-data) + connectivity. Order-routing dominates an HFT bill; market data is comparatively cheap (~$1k/yr for members).

The price of a millisecond: leased lines 82ms=$2k/mo → 78ms=$5k → 71ms=$45k → 68ms=$125k — the last 14 ms cost 60× more (~$8.8k per ms saved). TBT data: ~30-35 GB/day (NSE) vs few 100 GB (CME); NSE lists instruments in the 10,000s.

Regulation, compliance & operational risk

Trade via a broker → broker carries most compliance. As a member you own it:

  • Conformance test (e.g. CME CERT env) or strategy empanelment (India/Thailand — each automated strategy approved).
  • India retail API: <10 orders/s + static IP is exempt from approval.
  • Audit / transaction logs kept 4-8 years + saved control parameters.
  • US Pattern-Day-Trader: >4 intraday equity trades / 5 days → min $25,000 margin.
Operational risk > market risk

A cut fibre freezes market data while your engine fires on stale prices → a fast road to bankruptcy. Three guards: strategy-level staleness check, socket health, exchange heartbeat (loss triggers Cancel-on-Disconnect). Any guard failing → HALT & cancel orders.
