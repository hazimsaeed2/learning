# libs: pmdarima.auto_arima / sktime
# (here: AIC grid search over statsmodels)

AIC balances underfit (too simple, misses patterns) vs overfit (fits noise, fails on unseen data). Rewards fit, penalizes complexity — a scorecard.

💡 AIC optimizes fit, not profit — never compares to buy & hold. It often picks a simpler model that still loses.

⑥ ARCH & GARCH — forecast the vol

ARCH: σ²ₜ = ω + α·ε²ₜ₋₁
GARCH: σ²ₜ = ω + α·ε²ₜ₋₁ + β·σ²ₜ₋₁

ARCH (Engle, Nobel): variance from past shocks (squared returns proxy past variance). GARCH (Bollerslev): also feeds back past variance → captures clustering cheaply.

⏱️ 30-second glossary

AIC — information criterion balancing fit vs complexity.
auto_arima — searches (p,d,q), picks min-AIC.
Heteroscedasticity — variance changes over time.
Vol clustering — high vol follows high, low follows low.
ARCH / GARCH — forecast variance from shocks / + past variance.
ARIMA + GARCH — return forecast sized by risk forecast.
