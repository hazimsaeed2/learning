Execution Strategy II

Where, at what price, and in what size to place each slice — and how to not get manipulated.

THE PROBLEM
Client: "buy 30,000 MSFT by 3pm at best price." Where do you place each order?
YOUR GOAL
Beat VWAP, maximise expected value per slice, finish in time, avoid traps.
THE EDGE
Place by EV (not the touch), peg+discretion with a fair-price guard.

① The core example — grade your fill against VWAP

VWAP weights each interval's price by its volume. Buy below it = good execution. Microsoft session, 21 fifteen-minute marks:

StepCalcValue
Σ(price×vol)across the day28,639,943,075
Σ(vol)across the day30,879,500

Formula: VWAP = Σ(price×volume) / Σ(volume). Buy if price < VWAP, sell if price > VWAP.

② WHERE to place — expected value beats the touch

Closer to the touch → high fill chance, tiny gain. Deeper → big gain, rare fill. Optimise EV = P(fill) × gain. Level 2 wins, not level 1.

③ Discretion — a sure small edge > a coin-flip big one

A discretion order = limit order with a hidden, more-aggressive second price it pays only when a match appears. Fair price 100.25:

CaseEV = P × (fair − price)EV
1 — passive limit @100.000.50 × (100.25 − 100.00)0.125
2 — discretion lifts to 100.101.00 × (100.25 − 100.10)0.150 ◄

④ Blend pegging + discretion — and the fair-price GUARD

Pegging keeps your buy at best bid + 1 tick automatically. Discretion lets it lift to a match. Together they can cascade you into overpaying — so the last check before every order is buy price ≤ fair price.

⑤ Guerrilla bots / spoofing — why the guard matters

A spoofer baits a naive pegger (peg = best bid + 5c, no guard): posts tiny bids that drag you up, then sells into your inflated price and buys back low.

StepActionPrice
bait 1bot bids 100.2 → you chase100.25
bait 2bot bids 100.8 → you chase100.85
trapbot SELLS 1000 to you100.85
snap backbot BUYS back to cover100.50
P&L(100.85−100.50)×1000+$350 bot / −$350 you

Real case: Navinder Sarao spoofed ~$40M and was jailed. The fair-price guard (refuse anything above 100.5) makes the bait worthless.

⌛ 30-second glossary

  • VWAP — volume-weighted avg price; the execution benchmark.
  • Pegging — order auto-tracks a level (best bid + N ticks).
  • Discretion — hidden 2nd price the order will pay for a fill.
  • Fair price — your mid/model value; the hard guard line.
  • Same-side depth — cumulative qty down to a level.
  • Alone at level — only order at a price; may signal info.
  • Spoofing — fake orders to move price; illegal.
  • TTH — theoretical minutes to fill given the algo's share.
