Options Pricing Models & the Greeks

Premium = intrinsic + time value. Black-Scholes turns six inputs into a fair price; the Greeks are its partial derivatives — the dials a trader actually watches.

PREMIUM
intrinsic + extrinsic

S=100, 90-call @ $14 = $10 intrinsic + $4 time value

MONEYNESS
ITM · ATM · OTM

call ITM when S>X; put ITM when S<X

STYLE
American > European

exercise anytime = more optionality = dearer. Exotic: Asian (avg price)

PAYOFF ≠ P&L
subtract premium

payoff ignores cost; P&L starts in the red by the premium

PART 1 — COMBINATIONS

Protective put (hedge)

Long stock @ 90 + long 90-put @ $10. Below 90 the put offsets the stock — losses floored at the premium, upside open.

Bull call spread

Bullish but capped: buy 90-call, sell 120-call to recoup premium.

⚠ The 1×2 over-sell trap

Greedy: sell two 120-calls to grab more premium. The extra naked short bites once S runs past 120.

at S=140+$10
at S=150$0 → negative

Right direction, still a loss. Don't over-collect premium without an objective.

C−P = S−Xe⁻ʳᵀ

Proof: long put + short call + long stock = X at expiry whether the market finishes up (130) or down (80) — always 105. Only holds for European (needs fixed T).

Black-Scholes (the closed form exchanges use)

C = S·N(d₁) − X·e−rT·N(d₂)
d₁ = [ln(S/X) + (r + σ²/2)T] / (σ√T)
d₂ = d₁ − σ√T
N(d₁)= delta = hedge ratio (here 0.300)
N(d₂)= risk-neutral P(finish ITM) (0.271)

6 inputs: S, X, r, dividends, T, σ. No returns/drift — options look forward, BSM is risk-neutral.

Assumptions: continuous trading, constant σ (the big flaw), lognormal prices, risk-free r, European, no dividends, no costs.

Trader jargon & additivity

Greeks sum across a book. Sample: long 10 ATM calls + short 5 110-calls (×100):

net delta +460+$460 per $1 up-move
net theta −19.2−$19/day decay
net vega +122.8+$123 per +1% vol

"long 5000 delta" / "short 500 theta" / "long 1000 vega". 2nd-order: Vanna (∂delta/∂σ), Veta (∂vega/∂t) — build any Greek you need.

KEY FORMULAS

premium = intrinsic + extrinsic
intrinsic = max(S−X, 0)
C = S·N(d₁) − X·e−rT·N(d₂)
C − P = S − X·e−rT (parity)
delta = N(d₁);   delta_call − delta_put = 1
gamma = N'(d₁)/(S·σ·√T)
vega = S·N'(d₁)·√T
Greeks are additive across a portfolio
