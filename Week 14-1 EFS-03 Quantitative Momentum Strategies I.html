Quantitative Momentum I — Futures & Roll Returns

Momentum lives in futures. The engine is the roll return, not the noisy spot price.
Ernie Chan
QTS Capital · PredictNow.ai
THE PROBLEM
Finance has a tiny signal-to-noise ratio. Data-mining learns noise; you need to know why prices move.
YOUR GOAL
Build a causal model of momentum. Today: the roll return in futures — persistent, sometimes exact.
THE EDGE
Harvest the roll without predicting spot. ~50–60% accuracy is all you need to be in business.
THE DECOMPOSITION THREADING THE WHOLE SESSION
Total futures return = spot return + ROLL return
Spot is noise we refuse to predict. The roll is the causal, persistent edge. For the E-mini it's exact: roll = dividend − interest. The whole lecture is about reading the roll off the forward curve.

1 · The causal approach & three causes of momentum

Like physics: propose why prices move, then predict (Newton's gravity explains the apple and the orbits). A phenomenon ("prices trend") is not a model.

Tip: finance is so noisy a good causal model is right only ~50–60% of the time. Renaissance's edge is "in the decimals above 50%" — they just trade often. 55% is a sweet spot.
Cause of momentumWhere

Even passively holding SPY, you're moved by funds forced to buy/sell.

2 · The roll return, derived by arbitrage

$1m into SPY vs $1m notional ES future must give the same total return (else everyone trades one):

SPY = SPX + dividend
ES  = SPX + roll + interest
⇒ roll = dividend − interest

ES cash sits as margin and still earns interest (no funding cost); SPY uses up real cash. That interest term is the whole trick.

3 · Backwardation vs contango · why log returns

SignNameForward curve
roll > 0normal backwardationslopes down (front high)
roll < 0contangoslopes up (front low)
Keynes's wheat story: the farmer is long the crop → shorts futures to lock price → the speculator needs a positive roll to be enticed to go long. "Backwardation is normal." (Just a story — commodities do go into contango.)

Why log returns?

PropertyLogNet
Additive over time1%+2%=3%compounds (>3%)
Can be normal?yes (→ −∞)no (floored at −1)

Price can't go below 0, so a net return ≥ −1 can never be exactly normal; a log return can run to −∞.

4 · Linear model & the corn exercise — roll = −(slope of log forward curve)

ln F = α·t − γ·T

α = spot return, γ = roll return. Over a contract's life the slope is α+γ; on a forward curve (snapshot vs time-to-expiry T) the slope is −γ. So roll = −(slope of log forward curve).

5 · The causal payoff

The roll return is persistent — a backwardated future tends to stay backwardated, so its sign predicts the next period's roll. That persistence is the causal engine of CTA-style futures momentum.

You never predict the noisy spot price. You harvest the roll.

8 · Two backtest traps: roll gaps and mismatched timestamps

A continuous futures series is not created by simply joining expiring contracts. The price gap between the old and new contract is not real P&L, so a naive stitched series can create fake losses or gains. Use a back-adjusted series or explicitly model the roll.

The GC/GLD exercise also has a timestamp trap: GC settlement and GLD close may be observed at different times. A paired-market backtest must compare prices captured at the same timestamp; otherwise the spread can look profitable for a purely mechanical reason.

Sharpe rule: futures-only and dollar-neutral long/short strategies usually do not subtract the risk-free rate; short GC plus long GLD does, because buying GLD consumes cash.

30-second glossary

Roll return — futures return left after the spot move; total = spot + roll.
Spot return — the underlying index/cash move; treated as noise.
Backwardation — roll > 0; downward forward curve; "normal".
Contango — roll < 0; upward forward curve.
Forward curve / term structure — snapshot of contract prices vs time-to-expiry.
Arbitrage argument — two equivalent portfolios must give equal total return.
Carry — FX future roll = interest-rate differential.
Settlement price — VWAP of last minutes (or bid-ask mid), not last trade.
Month codes — F G H J K M N Q U V X Z = Jan…Dec.
