2️⃣ VIX FUTURES — Why −50% Roll Return (Theta), Regime Shift & Scatter Plot

WHAT: VIX Index vs VIX Futures vs VIX ETFs

⚠️ WHY YOU CAN'T REPLICATE VIX: VIX = snapshot of ATM implied vols. But ATM strike shifts every second as SPX moves. A portfolio mimicking VIX would need to rebalance every minute → impossible. Even if you did, the P&L of the dynamic portfolio ≠ VIX index return (snapshot vs holding). ALL VIX ETFs hold futures, never the index itself.

WHY −50% Roll Return (Theta Intuition)

Imagine holding options with constant VIX, constant SPX → nothing changes. Do you still lose money? YES — theta decay. Options lose value every day from time decay. This theta = VIX's contango. Insurance costs money even when nothing happens.

VIX range: 10-15% normal, 20-30% heightened, 100%+ extreme crisis.

4️⃣ SERIAL AUTOCORRELATION — Scanning for Momentum Candidates & the IID Trap

WHAT: Autocorrelation-Based Momentum Scan

Instead of causal models (hypothesis first), scan futures for positive autocorrelation of returns. Like an astronomer scanning the sky for anomalies before theorizing.

METHOD:

1. Pick look-back L (e.g., 250 days = 1 year)

2. Pick holding period H (e.g., 25 days = 1 month)

3. Compute return over L and return over H

4. Autocorrelation function → correlation + p-value

5. Filter: p-value < 0.05, then pick highest correlation

WORKED EXAMPLE: TU (2-Year Treasury Note Futures)

Look-back L250 days (1 year)
Holding H25 days (1 month)
Correlation0.27–0.29 (strong for finance)
p-value0.016–0.02 (statistically significant)
Sharpe≈ 1.0

💡 SIMPLEST MOMENTUM STRATEGY EVER:

If return_over_lookback > 0 → BUY

If return_over_lookback < 0 → SHORT

Hold for H days. Repeat.

Sharpe ≈ 1.0. Works on TU + at least 4 other futures.

THE IID VIOLATION (Critical Statistical Trap)

IID = Independent and Identically Distributed — the assumption behind almost every statistical test.

⚠️ PROBLEM: With L=250 lookback shifted by 1 day:

Sample 1: days [1-250] → return for [251-275]

Sample 2: days [2-251] → return for [252-276]

248 of 250 days OVERLAP → NOT independent!

p-value says 0.01 but reality is ~0.10

You think it's significant. It's NOT.

FIX: Non-Overlapping Samples

SOLUTION:

Shift by min(L, H) days, not by 1 day.

For L=250, H=25: shift by 25 days.

At least the holding period is non-overlapping.

Fewer samples, but honest p-values.

WHAT: p-value, Precisely

p-value = probability that the observed correlation is actually zero (a statistical fluke from too few data points). The null hypothesis is that there is no correlation.

Threshold: p < 0.05 → reject null hypothesis → correlation is "statistically significant."

Example: correlation = 0.75 from 3 data points → p = 0.50. Useless. Same correlation from 1000 non-overlapping samples → p = 0.001. Meaningful.

💡 NOT NORMAL, NOT STATIONARY: Many statistical tests do not need normality or stationarity here, but they do rely on IID-style independence assumptions. IID ≠ normality. IID ≠ stationarity. The independence part is what matters most in this lecture's warning.

5️⃣ MOMENTUM + MEAN REVERSION — The Best-of-Both-Worlds Combo (Sharpe 1.1)

THREE EQUITY CURVES

Combined ✓FLAT (0%)+great1.1

"You avoided all the pain, you're the hero." If you don't lose money during the crisis, you're already the best financial advisor.

HOW: Signal Alignment

TRADING RULES (CL futures example):

Momentum: price > price_40_days_ago → BUY

Mean rev: price < price_30_days_ago → BUY

Enter ONLY if BOTH signals agree.

(Use longer period for momentum, shorter for MR)

WHY signals align removes crash: During crisis, momentum says BUY (prices above 40-day), mean-reversion says SELL (prices above 30-day). They disagree → no position → FLAT during crisis.

During recovery: Both agree → enter position → capture upside.

ALTERNATIVE: Weighted Portfolio

Instead of signal alignment, trade both strategies independently:

Portfolio = 1 unit momentum + 2 units MR

This gives SOME crisis return (not zero) but also SOME drawdown during recovery. Adjust ratio to taste.

💡 MIRROR IMAGE: Momentum and mean reversion have EXACTLY opposite return profiles. Momentum = crisis alpha + crash. Mean reversion = crisis drawdown + recovery alpha. Combined = steady returns without extremes.

6️⃣ CALENDAR SPREAD — Why Linear Term Structure = Zero & The Curvature Requirement

WHAT: Calendar Spread Properties

Calendar spread = long one contract + short another contract (same underlying, different expiry). Spot return is perfectly hedged.

Term StructureSpread ReturnWhy
Linear (straight line)ZEROSame γ for all contracts → no differential to capture
Curved (non-linear)Can be > 0Different γ per contract creates something to harvest, but only if you take the correct front/back direction

RISK: Curvature can switch just like slope can switch (contango ↔ backwardation). If curvature flips, your spread loses money.

Gold may be a good candidate — curvature tends to be stable. Test it!

THE SIGN TRAP (Again!)

⚠️ EVERYONE GETS THIS WRONG:

Forward curve: steep negative slope at front,

less steep at back.

γ = −slope → front γ is MORE positive

→ back γ is LESS positive

Front has higher γ → you want to SHORT front?

NO! Slope is positive but steep at front:

γ = −(positive slope) = MORE NEGATIVE at front

→ SHORT FRONT + LONG BACK

The negative sign ALWAYS matters!

When Ernie asked the class, EVERY student said "buy front." All wrong. The negative sign of the forward-curve-to-roll-return mapping is the most common mistake in futures momentum.

💡 RULE: The front contract has the STEEPEST part of the forward curve. Steep positive slope = most negative γ = worst to hold long. Short the front, long the back.

7️⃣ LEVERAGED ETF MOMENTUM — Volatility Drag, Institutional Flows & Close-of-Day Signal

WHAT: Volatility Drag (Why 3× ≠ 3× Long-Term)

LONG-TERM GROWTH RATE:

g = μ − σ²/2

μ = arithmetic mean return

σ² = variance of returns

For 3× leveraged ETF (UPRO):

μ_UPRO = 3 × μ_SPY (by design, daily)

σ_UPRO = 3 × σ_SPY → σ²_UPRO = 9 × σ²_SPY

g_UPRO = 3μ − 9σ²/2

g_SPY = μ − σ²/2

⚠️ IF σ² IS LARGE ENOUGH: g_UPRO < g_SPY. The 3× ETF UNDERPERFORMS the 1× version long-term! Leverage amplifies variance 9× but return only 3×. The drag eats profits.

HOW: Institutional Rebalancing Creates Momentum

WHY 3× ETFs create momentum near market close:

To maintain 3× daily leverage, UPRO must rebalance near close:

Market up 2%Must BUY ~$32M of stock near close (on $270M portfolio)
Market down 2%Must SELL ~$32M of stock near close

Total levered assets tied to SPX = BILLIONS across all leveraged ETFs. All rebalance in the same direction in the last 15-30 minutes → massive end-of-day momentum flow.

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Zero risk = zero Sharpe (gold arb proves it). Imperfect hedges (USO/XLE, VX/ES) can generate Sharpe ≈ 1.0 because the hedge is intentionally imperfect: XLE is only an oil-sensitive equity proxy, not a direct oil hedge, and VIX's −50%/yr roll comes from theta decay of the underlying options complex.

2. Momentum crash is inevitable after crisis alpha. The Toronto hedge fund story: +$120M profit, then −$300M on inflated AUM; the firm did not survive that post-crisis profile. Four fixes: (1) stop at normal VIX, (2) drop short side, (3) stop-loss (doesn't work), (4) combine with mean reversion (Sharpe 1.1, avoids crash entirely).

3. Autocorrelation scanning finds momentum candidates (like TU) with the simplest possible rule: if lookback return > 0, buy; else short. But watch for IID violations — overlapping samples make p-values lie. Shift by min(L,H) days for honest statistics.

4. Calendar spreads need CURVATURE (not just slope) to profit — linear term structure = zero return. Leveraged ETFs create end-of-day momentum from billions in institutional rebalancing. Vol drag (g = μ − σ²/2) means 3× can underperform 1× long-term.

THE MASTER FORMULA: Risk = Return. Perfect hedge = zero Sharpe. Imperfect hedge = positive Sharpe. To profit from momentum, accept controlled risk (spot mismatch, curvature flip, regime shift) and hedge what you can.

THE ONE THING TO NEVER FORGET: "I'm not giving you a turnkey strategy." Every strategy needs bells & whistles — stop-loss, adaptive hedge ratios, regime detection, momentum+MR combo. The core idea is simple; the execution is where you earn your return. Back-test forward 14 years — "if it works and you don't trade it, it's your loss."
