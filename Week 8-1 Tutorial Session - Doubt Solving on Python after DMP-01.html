④ Windows paths & help

A backslash starts an escape sequence — use a raw string or doubled slashes:

pd.read_csv(r"C:\Users\me\data.csv")    # raw
pd.read_csv("C:\\Users\\me\\data.csv")  # doubled
Get help: yf.download? or help(yf.download); press Tab after a dot to list attributes.

⑤ Adjusted vs unadjusted close

Adj Close bakes in corporate actions — dividends, splits, issuances, mergers — applied backward so returns stay continuous. For an index (NIFTY-50) there are no splits, so Adj Close ≈ Close. Prefer adjusted for long-horizon work.

⑥ Time zones

yfinance intraday is UTC (+00:00). Convert to IST:

idx.tz_convert("Asia/Kolkata")  # 09:30 UTC -> 15:00 IST
pytz.all_timezones (or stdlib zoneinfo.available_timezones(), 598 names) lists every zone.

⑧ The SMA21/LMA63 crossover — 7 vectorized steps

d["SMA"] = d.Close.rolling(21).mean()  # 1 short MA
d["LMA"] = d.Close.rolling(63).mean()  # 2 long MA
d["regime"] = np.where(d.SMA>d.LMA,1,-1) # 3 long/short
d["signal"] = d.regime.diff()          # 4 +2 golden,-2 death
d["pos"]    = d.regime.shift(1)        # 5 no look-ahead
d["strat"]  = d.pos * d.Close.pct_change() # 6 returns
eq = (1 + d.strat).cumprod()           # 7 equity

Verdict: here the crossover beats buy-and-hold (68.04% vs 40.39%) — going short on the death cross dodged the 2020 COVID crash. Still just one back-test; validate with event-driven before trusting it.

⑨ Crossover logic

Golden cross (buy): short MA crosses above long MA — today SMA>LMA & yesterday SMA≤LMA. Death cross (sell/short): the reverse. regime.diff() spots the change: +2 up-cross, −2 down-cross. Shift the regime by one bar so trades land where they could actually execute.
