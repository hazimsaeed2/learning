Predict how, not where.
Two ways to edge
Model (daily, scalable) vs event (earnings/rates, rare). Pros use both.
Trader's bar

Why options

Adverse selection (the killer)

A limit buy that fills instantly means the seller was happy to dump on you. Winners sit at extreme prices & never fill; losers fill easily.

~80% of gross-profitable backtests die after costs. A student's Sharpe 1.45 → CAGR −2 net. Leverage raises costs.

Payoffs — payoff ≠ P&L

call = max(S−X, 0)  ·  put = max(X−S, 0)

Straddle = ATM call + ATM put. Profits on a big move either way, loses if pinned at strike. A long-volatility bet, not directional.

Volatility's three laws

LawValue
1 · neg. corr to index returns−0.68 (≈−0.7)
2 · vol-of-vol (level vs variability)+0.70 (≈+0.8)

Low vol → won't spike for no reason; high vol → a crash back to mean is likely. Gold flips Law 1 positive (safe haven).

ATM lowest; OTM puts richest (crash insurance + leverage). No single IV — varies by strike (smile) & expiry (term structure).

The payday — variance premium

VRP = IV − RV

IV almost always > RV because options are insurance. Harvest by selling rich vol.
