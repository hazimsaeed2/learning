3️⃣ THE 5 HIDDEN COSTS EXPLAINED — With Simple Analogies

1. DELAY COST 🟡

What: Price moves while you figure out which broker/algorithm to use.

When: BEFORE trading (from PD to P0)

Analogy: You see a TV on sale for $500. While you're comparing credit cards, the price goes to $530.

Our Example:

Decided at: $150.00

Sent order at: $150.30

Delay = $0.30/share

2. PRICE APPRECIATION 🔵

What: Stock naturally rises/falls WITHOUT your order.

When: DURING trading

Analogy: Boat in a river — the current moves you whether you paddle or not.

Impact:

• BUY + Rising market = BAD (pay more)

• BUY + Falling market = GOOD (pay less)

Can't avoid: Market moves on its own

3. MARKET IMPACT 🔵

What: YOUR buying pressure pushes price UP.

When: DURING trading

Analogy: You walk into a small store wanting ALL their apples. The owner sees your demand and raises prices.

Two Components:

• Temporary: Premium for speed (liquidity)

• Permanent: Others think you know something

Trade FAST → HIGH impact | Trade SLOW → LOW impact

4. TIMING RISK 🔵

What: Uncertainty/volatility while you're in the market. The longer you trade, the more unpredictable.

When: DURING trading

Analogy: Standing in a storm — the longer you're outside, the more uncertain your outcome.

Caused by:

• Price volatility (stock jumping around)

• Volume uncertainty (liquidity drying up)

Trade FAST → LOW risk | Trade SLOW → HIGH risk

5. OPPORTUNITY COST 🔴

What: Missed profit from shares you COULDN'T execute.

When: AFTER trading

Analogy: Concert tickets are $20. You wait. Now they're $35 and you can only get 8 of 10 tickets you wanted. You missed profit on 2 tickets.

Our Example:

Wanted: 100,000 shares | Got: 80,000

Missed: 20,000 shares

Price moved: $150.30 → $151.00 = $0.70

Lost: 20,000 × $0.70 = $14,000

4️⃣ IMPLEMENTATION SHORTFALL — Your TOTAL Trading Cost

STEP 1: PAPER RETURN

What you SHOULD have made if you bought ALL shares at decision price with NO fees:

Paper Return = S × (PN − PD)

S (total shares)100,000
PN (end price)$151.00
PD (decision price)$150.00

= 100,000 × ($151.00 − $150.00)

= 100,000 × $1.00

= $100,000

STEP 2: ACTUAL RETURN

What you REALLY made based on executed shares, your avg price, minus fees:

Actual = X × (PN − Pavg) − Commission

X (executed)80,000
PN − Pavg$151.00 − $150.80 = $0.20
Profit before fees80,000 × $0.20 = $16,000
Commission80,000 × $0.02 = $1,600

= $16,000 − $1,600

= $14,400

STEP 3: IMPLEMENTATION SHORTFALL

The TOTAL cost = what you lost to transaction costs:

IS = Paper Return − Actual Return

= $100,000 − $14,400

= $85,600

LOST TO TRANSACTION COSTS!

Now let's see WHERE this $85,600 went...

6️⃣ POST-TRADE METRICS — Did Your Broker Do a Good Job?

ARRIVAL COST

Cost during execution (in basis points)

= (Pavg − P0) / P0 × 10,000 × Side

What's a Basis Point?

1% = 100 bps | 0.01% = 1 bp

Decimal × 10,000 = bps

Our Example:

= ($150.80−$150.30)/$150.30 × 10,000 × 1

= 0.00333 × 10,000

= 33.27 bps

VWAP SLIPPAGE

How you did vs EVERYONE else

= (Pavg − VWAP) / VWAP × 10,000 × Side

Our Example:

VWAP = $150.70 (market average)

= ($150.80−$150.70)/$150.70 × 10,000 × 1

= 0.000664 × 10,000

= 6.64 bps

Meaning: You paid 6.64 bps MORE than the average trader. Not great!

RPM (Percentile Ranking)

What % of traders did you BEAT?

RPM = (Volhigher + ½×Volequal) / Total

Example:

Total volume: 1,000,000

Traded > $150.80: 300,000 (you beat)

Traded = $150.80: 100,000 (tied)

Traded < $150.80: 600,000 (beat you)

= (300,000 + 50,000) / 1,000,000

= 35%

Target: 40-60% is good. 35% = below average!

VALUE ADD

Did broker beat EXPECTATIONS?

Value Add = Expected Cost − Actual Cost

Example:

Expected: 50 bps

Actual: 33.27 bps

Value Add = 50 − 33.27

= +16.73 bps ✓

Meaning: Broker OUTPERFORMED by 16.73 bps!

Z-Score = Value Add ÷ Timing Risk

7️⃣ PRE-TRADE ANALYSIS — Forecasting Costs BEFORE You Trade

PRICE APPRECIATION FORECAST

Expected cost from market drift (stock moving on its own)

= Side × ½ × AlphaBP × Trade Time

Why ½? You buy throughout the day, not all at once. Your average purchase is at the midpoint.

Example Inputs:

AlphaBP100 bps
Trade Time1.0 (full day)
Side+1 (buy)

Calculation:

= 1 × ½ × 100 × 1.0

= 50 bps

TIMING RISK FORECAST

Uncertainty (standard deviation) of your cost estimate

= (σ / √250) × (1/3) × √[Size × (1−POV)/POV] × 10,000

Where:

• σ = Annualized volatility (decimal)

• 250 = Trading days per year

• 1/3 = Position shrinks as you trade

Key Insight:

• Trade LONGER → Higher timing risk

• Trade FASTER → Lower timing risk

🔑 MARKET IMPACT MODEL — The Holy Grail!

Expected cost from YOUR order moving the price. Brokers keep this SECRET!

Step 1: I-star (Instantaneous Impact)

I* = a₁ × Sizea₂ × Volatilitya₃

Step 2: Actual Market Impact (with your strategy)

MI = I* × [b₁ × POVa₄ + (1 − b₁)]

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Transaction costs are a DRAG on returns — always account for them

2. Hidden costs (below iceberg) are MUCH bigger than visible costs

3. Delay + Opportunity costs CAN be eliminated with preparation

4. NEVER trust broker marketing — calculate performance yourself!

THE MASTER FORMULA: Implementation Shortfall = Paper Return − Actual Return = Delay + Execution + Opportunity + Fixed Costs

THE TRADER'S DILEMMA: Trade FAST = High Impact + Low Risk | Trade SLOW = Low Impact + High Risk → Find the optimal balance!
