The Big Idea — the Swiss-Army Knife of Quant Trading

PCA finds the hidden structure in a basket of returns: which directions carry the most variance. One technique, dozens of uses — diversification meters, clustering, hedge ratios, factor portfolios.

Statistical arbitrage trades the spread between cointegrated assets. You can't predict where crude goes, but the crude-vs-gasoline gap is mean-reverting and therefore predictable.

Put them together and you get index arbitrage — a PCA eigen-portfolio traded against the index, the workhorse strategy that absorbs hundreds of millions without moving the market.

Golden rule of active management: IR = IC · √breadth — your Sharpe (IR) equals your skill (IC) times the square root of the number of uncorrelated bets. PCA is how you manufacture breadth.

PCA from Scratch — Three Small Steps

1
Demean returns

Centre each return series on zero — strips drift/trend. X = rets − rets.mean()

2
Covariance matrix

How each series varies and co-varies. S = X.cov() — diagonal = variances, off-diagonal symmetric.

3
Eigen-decomposition

vals, vecs = eig(S) — eigenvectors = principal components; biggest eigenvalue = biggest-variance direction.

Why PCA beats OLS for Hedge Ratios

OLS is not invertible

OLS minimises vertical distance, so swapping x and y does not give the reciprocal slope:

slope QQQ ~ SPY1.050
slope SPY ~ QQQ0.806
1 / 0.8061.240 ≠ 1.050

Order your pair differently → different hedge ratio → different P&L. Dangerous for stat-arb.

PCA is orientation-free

PCA minimises orthogonal distance to the line, so the first-component slope is the same whichever asset comes first.

PCA first-component slope = 1.154 (fixed)

Use PCA — not OLS — to size your pairs, and your strategy stops depending on an arbitrary choice.

Explained Variance & Clustering

① Explained variance = diversification meter

Normalise eigenvalues (sum to 1), sort, cumulate → how many components explain X% of variance. Six stocks from three sectors:

3 components ≈ 90% = the three sectors. A curve that jumps to 1 with one PC = poorly diversified (low breadth); a near-straight line = well diversified. Steepening can even warn of a crash (correlations → 1).

Statistical Arbitrage — Cointegration, not Correlation

The drunk & her dog

Each wanders randomly, but the distance between them is bounded and mean-reverting. Correlation is about returns moving together; cointegration is about a stationary spread — and a stationary spread is predictable.

Z-score each leg (subtract mean ÷ std). The 1/σ factor is the hedge ratio / position size, so the spread oscillates around zero.

spread = z(RB) − z(CL) → mean 0.00, std 0.32

Engle-Granger coint: t = −4.93, p = 0.0002 → cointegrated at 99%. Granger: CL→RB p ≈ 0.00003, RB→CL p = 0.055 → crude leads gasoline.

The pairs backtest (step logic)

SpreadAction
> +1short the spread (expect fall)
< −1long the spread (expect rise)
crosses 0exit at the mean
runs to ±3stop-loss

46 round-trips · +11.9 z-units · equity rises in steps

Warning: these strategies sit in drawdown most of the time — the spread keeps going past your threshold before reverting. Trade many pairs in parallel for breadth (and net the overlapping legs).

Index Arbitrage — PCA + Stat-Arb Together

① Eigen-portfolio

Run PCA on the index's biggest names; the first eigenvector = portfolio weights (a correlation-weighted portfolio).

weights 0.08–0.28 (tech heaviest)
corr w/ SPY = +0.994

② Hedge & spread

Regress index on the eigen-portfolio for the hedge ratio, form the spread, run the same pairs logic.

PC1 = 43.6% var
54 round-trips · 93% win

Top names are ultra-liquid → absorbs huge capital without moving the market.

③ Health checks

Engle-Granger cointegration t-stat ≈ −0.4 (p ≈ 0.97) → the test fails here.

Tom's view: the cointegration test is not predictive of future cointegration. Lean on economic reasoning + periodic re-fitting; guard against survivorship bias (use historical constituents).

PCA for CTA trend-following

The second principal component builds a portfolio orthogonal to the index — PC2 corr w/ index ≈ 0.00 (returns). Futures desks (≈130 contracts) synthesise fresh uncorrelated products from higher PCs and run trend-following on them, manufacturing breadth where the market gives little.

The eight takeaways

  1. Work in returns, demeaned — stationary, the right PCA input.
  2. PCA = 3 steps: demean → covariance → eigen-decomposition.
  3. Use PCA not OLS for hedge ratios (orientation-free).
  4. Explained variance = diversification/breadth meter (and crash early-warning).
  5. First two components cluster a universe instantly.
  6. Cointegration ≠ correlation — trade the stationary z-scored spread; expect long drawdowns; run many pairs.
  7. Index arb = first eigenvector as weights vs the index; higher PCs build uncorrelated products for trend-following.
  8. Guard against survivorship bias; don't trust the cointegration test alone — add economic reasoning & re-fitting.
