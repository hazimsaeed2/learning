THE PROBLEM
Part I gave the theory of roll returns. But a perfect hedge earns nothing (Sharpe 0).
YOUR GOAL
Harvest roll returns with an imperfect hedge that still earns — and survive the momentum crash.
THE EDGE
Crude, VIX and an autocorrelation scanner — momentum is crisis alpha: it pays in the panic.
THE THREAD: NO RISK → NO RETURN
short GC + long GLD → spot cancels → only roll left → roll ≈ rf → Sharpe 0
A flawless hedge has no idiosyncratic risk, so it earns only the risk-free rate, and Sharpe (which measures excess return) is zero. Every strategy today takes on some real risk to earn a real return. No free lunch.

1 · The oil strategy (USO/XLE) — crisis alpha, then the crash

Swap the perfect gold hedge for an imperfect one: trade the oil ETF USO against the energy-equity ETF XLE. Signal = the sign of the oil roll:

2 · The VIX/ES strategy — a −50% roll that is really theta

VIX = weighted avg implied vol of near-money ~1-month SPX options. You can't trade the index (its options reset every second). You trade the VIX future, whose roll is a brutal ≈ −50%/yr.

StepNumber
VIX index / front future15 / 16 (contango)
Monthly roll = (15−16)/16−6.2%/mo
Annualized−54%/yr
Why −50%? It's option theta. The underlying ~1-month options bleed time value daily even if spot and implied vol never move. Insurance is never free.

Hedge the spot with the E-mini (ES): VIX and ES are anti-correlated −0.75. Anti-correlated ⇒ sell BOTH or buy BOTH (NOT a classic long/short pairs trade).

Cardinal sin: never regress without a scatter plot first. The ES–VIX cloud splits into pre- and post-GFC regimes — fit only the post-crisis cluster (in finance, more data is not always better).

3 · Volatility-of-volatility — which regime suits which strategy

Post-GFC vol is a touch lower on average but its range is much wider — the volatility of volatility (index: VVIX).

StrategyWantsBetter regime
Mean reversionhigh VIX, low VVIX (steady swings)pre-crisis
Momentumvol expansion (breakouts run)post-crisis
Intuition: mean reversion shorts the high and buys the low — great on a steady sine wave, but a growing-amplitude wave keeps stopping you out. Momentum buys new highs — it loves the wave that keeps expanding. So a regime with rising vol-of-vol favours momentum; a calm, steady-swing regime favours mean reversion.

4 · Scanning for momentum — and the IID trap

Data-mine instead of theorize: test serial autocorrelation of returns over a look-back L and a hold H (classic: L=250, H=25). A correlation gives two numbers that matter: the coefficient and the p-value.

The IID trap: shifting the window by 1 day reuses 248/250 of the same data — samples are not independent, so the p-value is far too small (fake significance).
Samplingcorrp-value
every day (overlap)+0.300.000 (fake)
every min(L,H)=25 days+0.310.003 (honest)
Poor-man fix: sample every min(L,H) days so windows don't overlap. Keep only pairs with p<0.05, pick the highest corr. Simplest rule: look-back return > 0 → buy; < 0 → short; hold H days. Asness/AQR: skip the most recent month (use months 1–11), since the last month mean-reverts.

5 · Killing the crash · calendar spreads · levered-ETF momentum

COMBINE TO KILL THE CRASH

Trade only when slow momentum (price > 40d ago) and fast mean-reversion (price < 30d ago) agree.

Flat through the crisis, no pain, rides the recovery. Ernie: Sharpe ~1.1.

CALENDAR SPREAD = CURVATURE

Spot fully hedged → only roll left. On a straight log curve the roll is identical everywhere → spread earns 0. You need curvature.

Sign trap: roll = −slope. On a convex curve the front is steepest-down (most-negative roll) → short the front, buy the back (opposite of instinct). Risk: the curvature itself flips.
LEVERED-ETF MOMENTUM

3× ETF ≠ 3× annual return: g = μ − σ²/2, but leverage gives 3μ and 9σ² → volatility drag.

g
1× (μ8,σ30)+3.5%
3×−16.5%

To keep 3× exposure the fund buys highs, sells lows in the last 15 min (gamma) → tradable end-of-day momentum (Pauline Shum).

30-second glossary

Crisis alpha — momentum's outsized return during panics; it's a tail hedge.
Momentum crash — the long drawdown after the crisis hero year (Kent Daniel).
Roll return — future return minus index return; VIX future's is ~−50%/yr.
Theta — option time decay; the cause of the VIX future's negative roll.
Hedge ratio (β) — # of ES per VIX, in DOLLAR units (VX $1000 vs ES $50).
VVIX — volatility of volatility; high VVIX favours momentum.
IID — independent & identically distributed; overlapping windows break it.
Calendar spread — long one expiry, short another; needs curvature to earn.
Volatility drag — why 3× ETFs under-deliver: g = μ − σ²/2.
