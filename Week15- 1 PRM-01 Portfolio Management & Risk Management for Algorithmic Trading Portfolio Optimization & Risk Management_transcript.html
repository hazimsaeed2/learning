2️⃣ THE 5-PHASE RISK MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK — Structure → Identify → Evaluate → Limit → Design

PHASE 1: Structure & Policy

• Risk dept SEPARATE from trading dept (autonomy)

• Approval process for each new product/strategy

• Why: Kerviel knew compliance (back office) → hid $7B in unauthorized trades. Adoboli same path → $2.2B

• Rotation of team members: Mandatory 2-week leave. Some traders didn't take leave for 7-10 years to hide trades.

• Centralized PnL reconciliation across all desks

PHASE 2: Identify Sources of Risk

MarketPosition goes against you (price, vol)
Credit/CounterpartyBroker/exchange fails (2007 crisis)
FinancialFinancing dries up (LTCM: $100B AUM → bailout)
OperationalSystems, HDD full, virus, strategy theft
LiquidityCan't exit position (exogenous or endogenous)
RegulatoryShort-selling ban, margin increase, tariffs
Natural/PoliticalPandemic, war, terrorism

PHASE 3: Evaluate (Quantify)

Sensitivity analysis: Greeks (Δ, Γ, Θ, V), dividend risk, currency exposure (USD/INR 84→90 = +6 rupees without strategy)

What-if tables: Price × implied vol grid → portfolio value at each combo

VaR (Value at Risk): "8% prob. of 2% loss in 1 month." Methods: historical sim, Monte Carlo, covariance

CVaR (Conditional VaR): Expected loss BEYOND VaR threshold (fat tails)

Stress testing: Apply historical crises (2007, 2011 Eurozone, 2020 COVID, 2021 Russia-Ukraine, 2025 tariffs) to current portfolio

💡 VaR tells you about a bad day. CVaR tells you HOW bad that bad day gets. Stress tests tell you about black swan events.

WORKED EXAMPLE: Settlement Type Mismatch

Scenario: Options hedged with futures

Options P&L: +$5K (unrealized until expiry)

Futures P&L: −$5K (settled DAILY via M2M)

Net: $0 (hedged). But...

Futures loss hits your account TODAY.

Options profit won't arrive until expiry.

→ Margin call NOW, even though net position = $0

→ Forced to unwind at a loss

⚠️ GEOGRAPHY RISK: ABC company in Asia, 50% revenue from US. US imposes 200% tariff on non-US companies → revenue collapses → stock drops. Always check revenue geography, not HQ location.

⚠️ PIN RISK: At expiry, underlying = strike price. Put-call parity position: long call + short put + short underlying. If only one side gets exercised, the stock leg can still net out. The problem is the ATM uncertainty over whether both exercise decisions happen or not; that can leave you unexpectedly long or short stock overnight.

4️⃣ CASE STUDY DEEP DIVES — LTCM, Infinium, Deutsche Bank & Social Media Risks

LTCM (Long-Term Capital Management, 1998)

Founders: Myron Scholes + Robert Merton (Black-Scholes Nobel Laureates)

1995-96: +40% returns to investors (after costs)

AUM: $100B at peak

Strategy: bond arbitrage, HIGHLY leveraged

Trigger: Russian government defaulted on bonds

After default: had to sell at huge loss

No liquidity to unwind positions

Infinium Capital (24 Seconds, $850K)

Someone flipped it: ETF price → derive oil futures fair value → trade futures. Problem: ETF illiquid → fair value jumps wildly → futures orders filled instantly (liquid) → 4,612 trades in 24 seconds. RMS was still monitoring ETF, not futures → undetected.

5️⃣ ALGO TRADING SYSTEM RISK — Six Categories of Pre-Trade & In-Trade Controls

1. ACCESS & CONNECTIVITY

Heartbeats: Periodic "are you alive?" messages to exchange. If no response → pause trading.

Protocols: NTP (Network Time Protocol, ~ms) vs PTP (Precision Time Protocol, ~μs). TCPIP = seconds.

Check: MDA (Market Data Adapter) connectivity, OMS (Order Management System) connectivity, internal OS health.

2. CONSISTENCY (Stale Data)

Timestamp check: Exchange sends OHLC + timestamp. Compare with internal clock. If gap > threshold → discard.

Processing latency: Data arrives at 10:00:01 but reaches OMS at 10:01:00 → 59 seconds processing. If > threshold → discard order.

3. QUALITY (Garbled Data)

Bid > Ask: Some data providers offset prices for lower-tier subscribers. Bug → bid = 150, ask = 149. Check: bid must always be < ask.

Liquidity gaps: Before economic releases (US NFP), spread widens drastically (99.95/100 → 95/100). Pause trading during news windows.

4. ALGORITHM CONTROLS

Margin breach: If exceeded → pause immediately.

Order acknowledgment: With tick-by-tick data, don't send next order until previous is acknowledged.

CORRECT ORDER CHECK:

IF price(A) == 100

AND orders_placed(B) + orders_in_flight(B)

< MAX_ORDERS_THRESHOLD

THEN place_order(10 shares of B)

PnL fluctuation check: Sudden gains and sudden losses both need investigation. If the book suddenly shows +10% or −10%, verify whether the move is real or caused by a bad fair-value calculation, bad parameter, or another strategy bug.

Fat-finger and range checks: Validate parameters before launch, cap max traded value, and reject orders that are implausibly far from the last traded price for that asset.

⚠️ WITHOUT in-flight check: Tick arrives → order sent → next tick arrives BEFORE ack → another order sent → orders pile up exponentially with tick data.

💡 LOSS AVERSION: Humans feel $100 loss MORE than $100 gain (Kahneman). So we naturally check losses but MISS bugs that show fake profits. Check BOTH directions.

5. TECHNOLOGY

HDD full: Switching from seconds → tick-by-tick exhausted storage in HOURS. Multiple firms hit this.

System robustness: Watch for viruses, application crashes, hard-disk exhaustion, and protocol mismatches when multiple feeds or venues are involved.

6. SCALABILITY

Complexity growth: Strategy on 1 asset = O(1). On N assets = O(n). Nested loops = O(n²), O(n³). If processing > tick interval → stale data → wrong trades.

Capacity limits: Re-test whenever you add more symbols, portfolios, exchanges, or internal logic. A strategy that works on one asset can fail operationally when scaled across many.

🎯 KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. Risk management has 5 phases: structure (separate risk dept), identify (7 risk types), evaluate (VaR/CVaR/stress test), limit (8 limit types), and design (centralized controls). Skip any phase and you're Kerviel ($7B) or Knight Capital ($416M).

2. Every disaster has the same pattern: one missing check. Knight = no effective software version or integrity check. Infinium = RMS on wrong side. Deutsche = no stale detection. Credit Suisse = no confirmation. Build INDEPENDENT checks at every layer: MDA, CEP, and OMS.

3. With tick-by-tick data, always check orders_placed + orders_in_flight before sending the next order. Without this, every tick generates a new order before the previous is acknowledged → orders pile up exponentially. Infinium hit 4,612 trades in 24 seconds.

4. Check BOTH profits AND losses for anomalies. Humans are loss-averse (Kahneman) → we catch losses fast but miss bugs that show fake profits. A +10% spike is just as likely a bug as a −10% spike. Everbright didn't report their bug → banned forever.

THE MASTER FORMULA: Risk Framework = Structure (separate dept) + Identify (7 types) + Evaluate (VaR + CVaR + stress) + Limit (8 categories) + Design (MDA → CEP → OMS, each with independent checks).

THE ONE THING TO NEVER FORGET: Every single disaster in this lecture came from ONE missing check — software version, stale data, wrong instrument monitored, missing confirmation button. Your risk system must have redundant, independent checks at every layer. By the time a human notices, 4 million trades have already executed.
